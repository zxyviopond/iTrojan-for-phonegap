Title

Dean's Open Forum - Jaron Lanier, USC Annenberg's 2010 Innovator In Residence

Description

Join Dean Ernest J. Wilson III for a discussion on "Self and reality: Finding clarity in the digital noise" with Jaron Lanier, USC Annenberg's 2010 Innovator in Residence. From Lanier: "We humans have always been able to confuse ourselves by letting the means we use to communicate get ahead of us. We certainly can do this by talking, and don't even get me started about writing. When networked computers came along, media became a nocturnal, hyperactive thing that keeps on chattering even when we aren't listening. It makes up arguments to itself and then expects us to go along with them. It also follows us around everywhere. What are we to do with this peripatetic constant companion?" Lanier, author of "You are not a Gadget: Manifesto" (Knopf, 2010), blogger and columnist, computer scientist, composer and visual artist, currently serves as partner architect at Microsoft Research, which he has been affiliated with since 2006. Time magazine named Lanier one of the 100 most influential people in the world in 2010, and in 2005 Lanier was selected as one of the world's top 100 public intellectuals by readers of Prospect and Foreign Policy magazines. Commentary by communication professor Dmitri Williams.

Title

Media, Economics & Entrepreneurship @ USC Annenberg

Description

A major emphasis of USC Annenberg, the Media, Economics and Entrepreneurship initiative (M{2e} ) offers students both academic opportunities and hands-on research projects to strengthen their knowledge of economics. M{2e} infuses an understanding of economic principles and market behavior through course work across all of USC Annenberg's degree programs, encouraging students to innovate and experiment with new ideas for communication and journalism. Hear what USC Annenberg students, faculty and alumni are saying about the program.

Title

Eric Garland, Founder and CEO, BigChampagne Media Measurement

Description

AJoin communication professor Christopher Holmes Smith for a discussion with market researcher and entrepreneur Eric Garland, founder and CEO of BigChampagne Media Measurement. Garland's presentation, "Everything for Everyone, Everywhere, All the Time," will discuss the radical disruption of the music industry in the Internet age. More than a decade after Napster, illegal downloading dwarfs legitimate sales. Access beats ownership. The album format is falling apart and a multibillion dollar industry is collapsing. Garland will examine the diminishing value of entertainment media and the implications for anyone in the business of "content." From Garland: "We are witnessing a systemic collapse of nearly all of the fundamentals on which traditional media companies have depended for decades. Connected consumers are seizing ever more control and, in the process, virtually eliminating scarcity in information markets."
